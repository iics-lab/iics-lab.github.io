Principal Investigator

Zilu Liu · 刘孜路

Assistant Professor, School of Information Science and Technology, Great Bay University

Dr. Zilu Liu is an Assistant Professor in the School of Information Science and Technology at Great Bay University. Her research focuses on high-frequency and high-speed integrated circuits for AI computing, 6G communications, data-center interconnects, and biomedical systems, spanning SerDes ICs, low-jitter clocking ICs, millimeter-wave ICs, mixed-signal ICs, and AI-assisted IC design.

Research Directions

Chips, algorithms, and silicon feedback for intelligent interconnects

The lab studies the full path from circuit architecture and AI-assisted design to silicon measurement and system-level validation.

High-Speed SerDes and Clocking

Transmitters, receivers, CDR, PLL, equalization, jitter optimization, BER monitoring, and high-speed link validation.

AI-Assisted IC Design

Machine-learning surrogate models, active learning, automated parameter search, passive network optimization, and layout-aware prediction.

Intelligent Interconnect Systems

Adaptive calibration, channel-aware control, low-BER data links, chip-to-chip interfaces, and data-center interconnect systems.

RF/mm-Wave Front-Ends

Millimeter-wave transmitters, chip-antenna co-design, phased-array front-ends, power amplifiers, and broadband interfaces.

Silicon Validation and Data Feedback

Measurement-driven design loops, silicon debug, eye/jitter/power characterization, test boards, and data reuse for next-generation chips.

Integrated Systems for AI and Communications

Energy-efficient circuits and mixed-signal systems that connect sensing, computing, and communication in compact silicon platforms.
