Principal Investigator

Zilu Liu · 刘孜路

Assistant Professor, School of Information Science and Technology, Great Bay University

Dr. Zilu Liu received her Ph.D. in Electrical and Computer Engineering from The Hong Kong University of Science and Technology, and her B.Eng. in Information Engineering from South China University of Technology. Her research focuses on high-frequency, high-speed, and energy-efficient integrated circuits for AI computing, 6G communications, data-center interconnects, and biomedical systems.

Her work spans SerDes ICs, millimeter-wave transmitter front-ends and phased-array systems, low-jitter clocking circuits, AI-assisted IC design methodologies, and low-power mixed-signal ICs. Before joining Great Bay University, she worked as a Senior IC Engineer at Sanechips (ZTE), bringing practical chip-development experience and close collaborative ties with academia and industry. Her research has appeared in leading IEEE venues including IEEE T-MTT, JSSC, IMS, CICC, and ESSERC.

Research Directions

Integrated circuits for AI computing, 6G communications, and biomedical systems

The lab studies high-speed data movement, high-frequency front-ends, precise timing, and AI-assisted design methods, connecting early-stage methodology work with later chip demonstrations.

AI computing · Data centers · Chiplets

High-Speed SerDes and Wireline ICs

Energy-efficient transmitters, receivers, clock-data recovery, equalization, and adaptive link techniques for large-scale data movement across chips, boards, and systems.

6G · Satellite links · Wireless sensing

RF/mm-Wave ICs and Chip-Antenna Interfaces

Compact transmitter front-ends, phased-array building blocks, broadband matching networks, and chip-antenna co-design for high-frequency communication and sensing.

High-speed links · RF systems · Synchronization

Low-Jitter Clocking and Synchronization ICs

Low-jitter PLLs, clock recovery circuits, and synchronization techniques that support reliable data transmission under practical power and integration constraints.

Design automation · Modeling · Optimization

AI-Assisted IC Design Methodologies

Surrogate modeling, active-learning simulation loops, automated sizing, passive network optimization, and layout-aware prediction for efficient circuit design.

Wearables · Biomedical sensing · Health monitoring

Low-Power Mixed-Signal ICs for Biomedical Systems

Energy-efficient sensing interfaces and signal-processing circuits for compact, continuous, and intelligent biomedical and wearable platforms.
