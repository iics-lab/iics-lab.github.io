Openings

Join IICS Lab

We welcome motivated students and researchers who want to build integrated circuits for AI computing, 6G communications, data-center interconnects, and biomedical systems.

Open Positions

Students and researchers at different stages are welcome

IICS Lab is recruiting visiting students, research assistants, master students, Ph.D. students, and postdoctoral researchers. Formal degree applications should also follow the admission requirements of Great Bay University and the School of Information Science and Technology.

Visiting Students and Research Assistants

Suitable for students who want hands-on research experience in IC design, modeling, literature review, simulation, layout, test boards, or automation. Prior tape-out experience is helpful but not required.

Master and Ph.D. Students

Suitable for students who want to develop deep research projects in SerDes, RF/mm-wave circuits, PLL/CDR, AI-assisted IC design, or biomedical mixed-signal ICs, with a path toward publications and chip demonstrations.

Postdoctoral Researchers

Suitable for researchers who want to lead chip projects, mentor students, build collaborations, and develop a strong publication pipeline around high-speed, high-frequency, and intelligent IC systems.

Useful Backgrounds

Integrated circuits, analog/RF design, high-speed links, signal integrity, mixed-signal systems, electromagnetics, machine learning, EDA, scientific computing, embedded systems, or measurement automation.

Research Areas

Possible project directions

Projects are usually connected to the lab's five research directions and can range from early AI-assisted methodology work to later circuit implementation and chip demonstration.

  • High-Speed SerDes and Wireline ICs: transmitters, receivers, equalization, clock-data recovery, and adaptive links for AI computing and data centers.
  • RF/mm-Wave ICs and Chip-Antenna Interfaces: millimeter-wave transmitter front-ends, phased-array building blocks, broadband matching, and 6G or satellite-link applications.
  • Low-Jitter Clocking and Synchronization ICs: PLLs, CDR circuits, jitter optimization, phase-noise reduction, and clocking for high-speed links and RF systems.
  • AI-Assisted IC Design Methodologies: surrogate modeling, active-learning simulation loops, automated sizing, passive-network optimization, and layout-aware prediction.
  • Low-Power Mixed-Signal ICs for Biomedical Systems: sensing interfaces, biomedical signal acquisition, wearable systems, and energy-efficient mixed-signal circuits.

Application

How to apply

Please email the PI with a concise subject line and enough material to understand your background, interests, and possible fit with the lab.

  • Email subject: [IICS Application] Position - Your Name.
  • Attach a CV or resume, transcripts if available, and a brief statement of research interests.
  • Mention one or two preferred research directions and why they interest you.
  • For IC-design applicants, include project summaries, layouts, simulation results, measurement experience, or tape-out details if available and shareable.
  • For AI/EDA or automation applicants, include code examples, papers, project reports, or datasets if available.
  • Send materials to liuzl@gbu.edu.cn and optionally copy elsie.lll@outlook.com.
