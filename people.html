Principal Investigator

Zilu Liu · 刘孜路

Assistant Professor, School of Information Science and Technology, Great Bay University

Assistant Professor, School of Information Science and Technology, Great Bay University | Ph.D., The Hong Kong University of Science and Technology

IC Design for: AI Computing | 6G Communications | Data-Center Interconnects | Biomedical and Wearable Systems

Research Topics: SerDes ICs | Low-Jitter Clocking ICs | Millimeter-Wave ICs | Mixed-Signal ICs | AI-Assisted IC Design

Dr. Zilu Liu is an Assistant Professor in the School of Information Science and Technology at Great Bay University. She received her Ph.D. in Electrical and Computer Engineering from The Hong Kong University of Science and Technology, and her B.Eng. in Information Engineering from South China University of Technology.

Her research focuses on high-frequency and high-speed integrated circuits for AI computing, 6G communications, data-center interconnects, and biomedical systems. She develops circuit architectures and design methodologies that enable faster, more energy-efficient, and more reliable data movement across chips, boards, and wireless links.

Before joining Great Bay University, Dr. Liu worked as a Senior IC Engineer at Sanechips (ZTE), where she contributed to advanced high-speed SerDes transmitter front-end development. Her experience connects academic circuit innovation with practical chip development, and she maintains close academic and industrial collaborations with leading universities and IC companies, including HKUST, The Chinese University of Hong Kong, Sun Yat-sen University, Huawei, and ZTE.

Dr. Liu's representative work includes millimeter-wave transmitter front-ends and phased-array systems for next-generation wireless and satellite communications, low-jitter clocking circuits for high-speed links, high-speed interconnect circuits for AI computing and data-center systems, AI-assisted circuit design methods for passive networks, and low-power mixed-signal circuits for biomedical and wearable applications.

Her research has been published in leading IEEE journals and conferences, including IEEE T-MTT, IEEE JSSC, IEEE IMS, IEEE CICC, and IEEE ESSERC. Her IEEE T-MTT paper was selected as a Featured Article, with only five papers selected from more than one hundred papers in each issue. Related low-jitter clocking work was invited to IEEE JSSC.
