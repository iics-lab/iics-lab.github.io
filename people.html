Profile

Dr. Zilu Liu received her Ph.D. in Electrical and Computer Engineering from The Hong Kong University of Science and Technology, and her B.Eng. in Information Engineering from South China University of Technology.

Her research focuses on high-frequency, high-speed, and energy-efficient integrated circuits for AI computing, 6G communications, data-center interconnects, and biomedical systems. She develops circuit architectures and design methodologies that enable faster, more reliable, and more energy-efficient data movement across chips, boards, and wireless links.

Before joining Great Bay University, Dr. Liu worked as a Senior IC Engineer at Sanechips (ZTE), where she contributed to advanced high-speed SerDes transmitter front-end development. Her experience bridges academic circuit innovation and practical chip development, and she maintains close collaborative ties with leading universities and IC companies, including HKUST, The Chinese University of Hong Kong, Sun Yat-sen University, Huawei, and ZTE.

Dr. Liu's representative work includes millimeter-wave transmitter front-ends and phased-array systems for next-generation wireless and satellite communications, low-jitter clocking circuits for high-speed links, high-speed interconnect circuits for AI computing and data-center systems, AI-assisted circuit design methods for passive networks, and low-power mixed-signal circuits for biomedical and wearable applications.

Her research has been published in leading IEEE journals and conferences, including IEEE T-MTT, IEEE JSSC, IEEE IMS, IEEE CICC, and IEEE ESSERC. Her IEEE T-MTT paper was selected as a Featured Article, with only five papers selected in each issue out of more than one hundred published articles. Related low-jitter clocking work was invited to IEEE JSSC.
