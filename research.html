Research

Intelligent interconnects from circuit ideas to silicon data

IICS Lab studies integrated circuits and systems that make data movement faster, cleaner, more adaptive, and easier to design.

1. High-Speed SerDes and Wireline Links

We investigate transmitter and receiver front-ends, equalization, CDR, BIST, BER monitoring, and link-level validation for next-generation chip-to-chip and data-center interconnects.

2. Low-Jitter Clocking and Synchronization

The lab develops PLL/CDR architectures, jitter optimization methods, clock recovery circuits, and monitoring techniques for high-speed interfaces.

3. AI-Assisted IC Design Automation

We build machine-learning surrogate models, active-learning simulation loops, automated circuit sizing methods, passive-network optimization, and layout-aware prediction flows.

4. Intelligent Calibration and Low-BER Adaptation

We explore channel-state recognition, BER prediction, adaptive equalization, background calibration, and data-driven control for robust links under changing channel and process conditions.

5. RF/mm-Wave Front-Ends and Chip-Antenna Interfaces

We study compact mm-wave transmitter front-ends, phased-array circuits, broadband matching networks, and chip-to-antenna co-design for future wireless systems.

6. Silicon Validation and Data Feedback

We turn measurement results into reusable design knowledge through silicon characterization, eye/jitter/power testing, test boards, and feedback loops for the next tape-out.

Research Philosophy

Closed-loop IC research

Our long-term goal is to connect AI-assisted design, high-speed circuit implementation, silicon measurement, and system-level adaptation into a practical loop.

  • Design: circuit architecture, modeling, simulation, layout, and verification.
  • Tape-out: compact test chips and reusable IP for clocking, links, and calibration.
  • Measure: jitter, eye diagrams, BER, power, bandwidth, and system robustness.
  • Learn: use silicon data to improve models, algorithms, and the next design cycle.
